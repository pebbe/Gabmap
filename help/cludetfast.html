This methods works on the individual responses in the data. The result may be that we are comparing scores for items based on data that covers quite different areas, due to missing data. So, the scores may perhaps not be comparable. This may lead to a suboptimal ranking of the items. It doesn't effect the selection of patterns within an item.

Is this a problem? You don't need the values to be accurate. All you need is a sensible ranking. You may get some high-ranking items that don't lead you to a clear, determining feature. But this may happen even in the localised data method, perhaps not as often. If it happens, just move on to the next item.

Different items can have different amounts of data, resulting in different error margins, so we correct for the median expected error, in order to get a more reliable comparison between items. We do this by adding 1 to each count of true positives, false positives, false negatives, and true negatives. This method is based on the predictive values of small sample draws from large data sets.
